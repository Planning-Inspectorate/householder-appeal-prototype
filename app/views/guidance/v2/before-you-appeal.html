Before you appeal

If your householder planning application has been refused by the local planning department, you can:​

  • submit an amended application to the local planning department
  • appeal the decision with the Planning Inspectorate​

Amending the planning application

The decision letter from the local planning department will list the reasons for refusal. If you think you can change your plans to respond to them, speak to the planning department about resubmitting your application.

Your local planning department (also called ‘LPA’) is usually your local council. The contact details will be on the decision letter.

Appealing the decision

Around 1 in 3 appeals is successful. You should only appeal if you think all the reasons for refusing your application are incorrect.

The Planning Inspectorate is an independent, impartial body that decides appeals on planning applications.

When you appeal, the Planning Inspectorate will look at the planning considerations from you (set out in your appeal statement) and the local planning department in order to make a decision.

There’s no fee for appealing. ​

Commenting on an appeal

Unlike a full planning appeal, no one can comment on a householder planning appeal.

If anyone commented on the original planning application ('interested party representations'), the local planning department will tell them that there is an appeal, but they won't be able to comment on it.​

Applying for costs if any party behaves unreasonably

You can apply for an ‘award of costs’ at the same time you appeal, if any party involved in your appeal has cost you money by behaving unreasonably, for example by missing deadlines. You can have costs awarded against you as well.
