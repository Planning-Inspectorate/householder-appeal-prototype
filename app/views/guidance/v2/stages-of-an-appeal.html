The stages of an appeal

1. Submit appeal

Start your appeal using the online service. You’ll need to have your original planning application details to hand.

After submission you will need to send a copy of your appeal to the local planning department.

2. The local planning department submit their case file

Maidstone Borough Council will send you a copy of their questionnaire and supporting documents for your information only, you do not need to do anything.

3. Site visit

One of our Inspectors will need to visit the appeal site. We'll be in touch if the Inspector needs you to provide access.

4. Decision

The Inspector will consider the evidence from all parties to make a decision.

You can expect to get a decision on your appeal in around 8 weeks.

How your appeal will be decided​

Householder planning appeals are always decided by written representations, which means the Inspector looks at written evidence from all parties to reach a decision. ​
