When you can appeal

You can appeal a householder planning decision from your local planning department if you think all their reasons for refusing your planning application are incorrect.

The appeal must be in the same name as the planning application. If you applied on behalf of a business or organisation, the appeal should be in the name of that organisation.​

There is a different process to appeal:

  • a full planning decision
  • a listed building appeal
  • a high hedges decision
  • a tree preservation consent order decision
  • an enforcement notice

Deadline for appealing​

You must appeal within 12 weeks of the decision date on the letter from your local planning department. If you cannot use the online service, you must leave enough time for us before the deadline to help you complete the process.

The deadline is earlier if you’ve received an enforcement notice for the same, or very similar, development. In which case you must appeal within 28 days of the notice through the main appeals service.
