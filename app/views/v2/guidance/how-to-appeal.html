How to appeal

You can appeal a householder planning decision online.

You need to send a copy of your appeal, including all the supporting documents, to your Local Planning Authority. The Planning Inspectorate will tell you how to do this.​

The stages of an appeal

How your appeal will be decided​

Householder planning appeals are always decided by written representations, which means the planning inspector looks at written evidence from all parties to reach a decision.

Documents you must provide​

You’ll need to upload copies of:

  • your original planning application
  • the Local Planning Authority’s decision notice​
  • your appeal statement​

How to write your appeal statement

You can upload these documents when you appeal.​

There’s more guidance on the appeals website and appeals form about which documents to submit.

Commenting on an appeal​

Unlike a full planning appeal, no one can comment on a householder planning appeal. Your Local Planning Authority must tell anyone who has commented on the original application (‘interested parties’) that there’s an appeal.​
