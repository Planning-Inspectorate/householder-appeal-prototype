Before you appeal

If your householder planning application has been rejected by the Local Planning Authority (LPA), you can either:​

  • submit an amended application to the LPA​
  • appeal the decision with the Planning Inspectorate​

Amending the householder planning application​

The LPA’s decision notice will list the reasons for refusal. If you think you can change your plans to respond to these, speak to the planning case officer at the LPA about resubmitting your application.

Submitting an appeal​

You should only appeal if you think the LPA’s decision to refuse your application was incorrect. Only around 1 in 3 appeals are successful, so you should be confident that you have a reasonable case for appeal before you start the process.​

When you appeal, the Planning Inspectorate will look at the planning considerations from all parties, including the LPA, the applicant and interested parties.

There’s no fee for appealing.​
