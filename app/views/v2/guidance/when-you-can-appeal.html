When you can appeal

You can appeal a householder planning decision from your Local Planning Authority (LPA) if you think the LPA’s decision was incorrect.

You must have made the original planning application yourself. If you applied on behalf of a business or organisation, the appeal should be in the name of that organisation.​

There’s a different process to appeal a full planning decision.​

Deadline for appealing​

If you disagree with a decision, You must appeal within 12 weeks of the date on the decision notice from your LPA.​

The deadline’s earlier if you’ve received an enforcement notice - you must appeal within 28 days of the notice.​

Checking your appeal​

The Planning Inspectorate will need to make sure your appeal is valid, which includes checking it contains all the required documents. This should around 2 weeks, but may take longer in busy periods.

Once we’ve checked that your appeal is valid, we’ll send you a letter confirming that. This letter gives more detail on how long planning appeal decisions normally take.​
