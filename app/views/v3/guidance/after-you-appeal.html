After you submit your appeal

You’ll be contacted once the Planning Inspectorate has checked that your appeal is valid. The next step is for your appeal to be assigned to an Inspector – this may take some time.​

Check how long householder planning appeal decisions may take.​

If any party behaves unreasonably​

You can apply for an ‘award of costs’ if any party involved in your appeal has cost you money by behaving unreasonably, for example missing deadlines. You can have costs awarded against you as well.​

You can complain about how the Planning Inspectorate handled your appeal. There’s no time limit for complaints.

If you disagree with the appeal decision​

If you think the Planning Inspectorate made a legal mistake, you can challenge the decision in the High Court.​

Get advice from a lawyer if you’re unsure about this.​
